Odin's Table: Viking Mead Recipe

Image Source= Batchmead.com

Recipe: Courtesy of Easymead.com

This recipe for "Vikings Blood" Mead does not actually contain any blood, but the color comes from the addition of fruit and hibiscus which adds flavor, aroma, and depth to round out the sweetness of honey.

For this 5 gallon recipe you will need:

Equipment

  • 5 gallon Fermenter
  • Brewing Paddle
  • Airlock
  • Siphon
  • Bottles

Ingredients:

  • 4 gallons Spring Water
  • 15.7 lbs Honey
  • 6 lbs cherries
  • 12 oz Hibiscus
  • 2 packs D47 Mead Yeast
  • 12.5 grams Go Ferm Tablets
  • 23.5 grams Fermaid (Yeast Nutrient)
  • x5 Campden Tablets
  • Sorbate

Instructions

  1. Make a hibiscus tea by steeping the dry hibiscus in boiling water
  2. Prepare a yeast starter with the Go Ferm and yeast
  3. Add the tea, honey, berries and half of the water to the fermenter
  4. Use a brew paddle to dissolve the honey in the liquid
  5. Add the rest of the water to the desired volume/SG
  6. Measure the specific gravity
  7. Add pectic enzyme
  8. Add yeast nutrients and pitch yeast
  9. Attach airlock and ferment
  10. Monitor, bottle and age
